<h2 class="font-section-header text-center text-on-background mb-stack-lg uppercase tracking-widest">GLUCOMOOD TIMELINE</h2>

<div class="max-w-4xl mx-auto relative pl-12 space-y-16">

<!-- Step 1 -->

<div class="relative">

<div class="absolute -left-[52px] w-10 h-10 bg-primary text-on-primary rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl z-10 shadow-lg shadow-primary/20">1</div>

<div>

<h3 class="font-section-header text-2xl text-on-background mb-3">The Problem with Dexcom</h3>

<p class="font-lead-text text-secondary opacity-90">GlucoMood started from my own experience wearing a Dexcom and living with the reality of checking glucose data regularly. I kept thinking that something so tied to how you feel physically and emotionally should not have to look so plain, clinical, and disconnected from real life.</p>

</div>

</div>

<!-- Step 2 -->

<div class="relative">

<div class="absolute -left-[52px] w-10 h-10 bg-primary text-on-primary rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl z-10 shadow-lg shadow-primary/20">2</div>

<div>

<h3 class="font-section-header text-2xl text-on-background mb-3">Humanizing the Data</h3>

<p class="font-lead-text text-secondary opacity-90">A lot of health apps feel like they only care about showing numbers, but not the person behind those numbers. As someone with ADHD, I knew I wanted something that felt more visually engaging, more alive, and easier to emotionally connect with, because design really affects whether something feels supportive or draining.</p>

</div>

</div>

<!-- Step 3 -->

<div class="relative">

<div class="absolute -left-[52px] w-10 h-10 bg-primary text-on-primary rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl z-10 shadow-lg shadow-primary/20">3</div>

<div>

<h3 class="font-section-header text-2xl text-on-background mb-3">The Mood Connection</h3>

<p class="font-lead-text text-secondary opacity-90">That is where the idea for GlucoMood came from: a glucose-tracking app that feels more vibrant, mood-aware, and human. I wanted to build something that still respected the seriousness of health data, but did it in a way that felt more personal and expressive instead of cold and sterile.</p>

</div>

</div>

<!-- Step 4 -->

<div class="relative">

<div class="absolute -left-[52px] w-10 h-10 bg-primary text-on-primary rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl z-10 shadow-lg shadow-primary/20">4</div>

<div>

<h3 class="font-section-header text-2xl text-on-background mb-3">A New Visual Language</h3>

<p class="font-lead-text text-secondary opacity-90">From the beginning, my goal was not just to make a prettier Dexcom-style app. I wanted to create a space where glucose data, mood, energy, and day-to-day patterns could feel more connected, so the experience made more sense as part of someone’s actual life.</p>

</div>

</div>

<!-- Step 5 -->

<div class="relative">

<div class="absolute -left-[52px] w-10 h-10 bg-primary text-on-primary rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl z-10 shadow-lg shadow-primary/20">5</div>

<div>

<h3 class="font-section-header text-2xl text-on-background mb-3">Emotive States</h3>

<p class="font-lead-text text-secondary opacity-90">This project feels personal because it comes from something I actually wear on my body and think about in real life, not just a random app idea. I wanted to design from that place of lived experience, where the product feels like it understands the emotional side of tracking health too.</p>

</div>

</div>

<!-- Step 6 (AI Layer) -->

<div class="relative">

<div class="absolute -left-[52px] w-10 h-10 bg-primary text-on-primary rounded-full flex items-center justify-center font-bold text-xl z-10 shadow-lg shadow-primary/20">6</div>

<h3 class="font-section-header text-2xl text-on-background mb-3">Mimi: The AI Companion</h3>

<p class="font-lead-text text-secondary opacity-90">One of the biggest future parts of GlucoMood is the AI assistant. My vision is for it to help users during high or low sugar moments, especially after hours when someone might be overwhelmed, scared, or unsure what to do next, while being very clear that it is there for support and guidance, not to replace a doctor or medical care.</p>

</div>

<h3 class="font-section-header text-2xl text-on-background mb-3">Metabolic Intelligence</h3>
